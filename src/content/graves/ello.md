# Ello

An ad-free social network that went viral in 2014 amid Facebook privacy controversies. Promised never to sell user data but failed to build features compelling enough to keep users after the initial hype.

## The Rise

**2014-03** — Launched as an invite-only social network by artist and designer Paul Budnitz

**2014-09** — Goes viral during Facebook real-name policy controversy; 31,000 invite requests per hour

**2014-10** — Raises $5.5M Series A

## The Fall

**2015-01** — Opens to public; user engagement drops sharply

**2015-06** — Pivots to creative community platform for artists and designers

**2016-01** — Lays off staff; Burlington office closes

**2018-06** — Effectively abandoned; site still online but no active development

## Key Lessons

### Privacy is not a product feature

Ello's entire value proposition was 'we're not Facebook.' But users don't switch platforms for absence of features — they switch for presence of better ones.

### Hype without retention is just noise

31,000 invite requests per hour sounds impressive. But when the invite wall came down, most users tried it once and left.

### Anti-brand positioning limits growth

By defining itself solely against Facebook, Ello had no independent identity. When Facebook controversies faded, so did Ello's relevance.
