# QuadrigaCX

Canada's largest cryptocurrency exchange. Collapsed in 2019 when founder Gerald Cotten died under mysterious circumstances in India, allegedly taking the only passwords to $190M in customer cryptocurrency with him. Later investigations revealed it was a long-running fraud.

## The Rise

**2013-11** — Founded by Gerald Cotten and Michael Patryn in Vancouver

**2017-01** — Becomes Canada's largest crypto exchange during Bitcoin bull run

**2018-01** — Platform experiences withdrawal delays; Cotten claims technical issues

**2018-12** — Gerald Cotten reportedly dies of Crohn's disease complications in India

## The Fall

**2019-01** — Quadriga freezes withdrawals; customers learn Cotten held sole access to cold wallets

**2019-02** — Files for bankruptcy protection; $190M missing

**2019-06** — Investigators reveal Cotten had been operating a Ponzi scheme since 2016

**2022-06** — EY reports $46M recovered; most funds remain missing

## Key Lessons

### Never give one person sole access to customer funds

Quadriga's 'cold wallet' was controlled exclusively by Cotten. No multi-sig, no backup, no corporate governance. A single point of failure by design.

### Death in India should raise red flags

Cotten died in a country where death certificates are forgeable. His death was suspicious from day one. The company had no continuity plan.

### Exchanges need proof of reserves

Quadriga operated for years without any external audit of its holdings. Customers trusted the brand without verifying the assets existed.
