# Google Wave

Google's ambitious real-time collaboration platform that blended email, instant messaging, wikis, and documents. Brilliant technology that arrived a decade before the world was ready for Slack and Notion.

## The Rise

**2007-01** — Rasmussen brothers begin developing 'Walkabout' prototype in Australia

**2009-05** — Announced at Google I/O to massive hype and standing ovation

**2009-09** — Invites 100,000 new users; only 27,000 become active

## The Fall

**2010-05** — Officially released with group functionality and notifications

**2010-08** — Google announces it will stop developing Wave as a standalone product

**2012-04** — Servers shut down; open-source Apache Wave also eventually dies in 2018

## Key Lessons

### Positioning is everything

Wave couldn't answer 'what is it?' Was it email? Chat? A document? Users were confused from day one.

### Don't limit early access too much

Restricting to 6,000 developers initially killed network effects. By the time it opened up, hype had faded.

### Being 10 years too early is still too early

Wave's core concepts — real-time collaboration, shared workspaces, embedded media — are now standard in Slack and Notion.
