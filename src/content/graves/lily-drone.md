# Lily Drone

A selfie drone startup that generated massive buzz with a promotional video showing the drone autonomously following users. Raised $15M in pre-orders but never delivered a product, eventually shutting down without refunding customers.

## The Rise

**2013-09** — Founded by UC Berkeley robotics students

**2015-05** — Promotional video goes viral; $15M in pre-orders in 6 months

**2015-12** — Promised shipping date of February 2016 passes without product

## The Fall

**2016-06** — Delays continue; admits video was partially staged with a GoPro

**2016-12** — Still no shipping date; burns through funding on R&D

**2017-01** — Announces closure; most customers never receive refunds

## Key Lessons

### Marketing videos are not proof of concept

Lily's famous promo video was partially staged. The actual drone couldn't achieve the autonomous tracking shown.

### Pre-orders are not revenue

$15M in pre-orders created false confidence. The company spent the money on R&D but couldn't manufacture a working product.

### Autonomous flight is harder than it looks

Following a person while avoiding obstacles requires advanced computer vision. Lily's team underestimated the technical challenge.
