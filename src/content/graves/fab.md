# Fab.com

A flash-sales site for design products that raised $310M and was valued at $1 billion. Died after multiple failed pivots from flash sales to full-price retail to its own inventory model.

## The Rise

**2010-06** — Launched as Fabulis, a gay social network

**2011-03** — Pivots to Fab.com, a flash-sales site for design products

**2011-12** — Hits 1 million members; $20M in sales

**2013-06** — Valued at $1 billion after Series C

## The Fall

**2013-11** — Pivots from flash sales to full-price everyday store — alienating core customers

**2014-01** — Lays off hundreds of employees; exits European markets

**2014-06** — Acquires custom furniture startup; pivots again

**2015-03** — Sells assets to PCH International for undisclosed sum; effectively worthless

## Key Lessons

### One pivot is risky; three pivots is death

Fab pivoted from social network to flash sales to full-price retail to custom furniture. Each pivot burned cash and confused customers.

### Flash sales are not a sustainable business model

The model depends on constant novelty and scarcity. When Fab tried to become a regular store, it had no competitive advantage over Amazon.

### Raising too much money creates pressure to spend it

$310M forced Fab to pursue massive growth. When growth stalled, the burn rate became unsustainable.
