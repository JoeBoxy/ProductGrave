# Palm / webOS

The pioneer of handheld computing with the PalmPilot and later the groundbreaking webOS. HP acquired Palm for $1.2 billion in 2010 and effectively killed it within 12 months through corporate incompetence.

## The Rise

**1996-01** — PalmPilot 1000 launches; becomes instant hit

**2000-03** — Palm IPO; valued at $53 billion on first day

**2007-01** — Apple announces iPhone; Palm begins webOS development in secret

## The Fall

**2009-01** — Palm Pre launched with critically acclaimed webOS at CES

**2010-04** — HP acquires Palm for $1.2 billion

**2011-02** — HP announces it will discontinue Palm hardware and license webOS

**2011-08** — HP kills webOS hardware division entirely

## Key Lessons

### Great OS, terrible business execution

webOS was praised as better than iOS by critics. But Palm had no money to market it or build an app ecosystem.

### Corporate acquisitions often destroy innovation

HP bought Palm for $1.2B and killed it within a year. The founders left, the talent scattered, and webOS became open-source abandonware.

### Carrier relationships matter

The Pre launched exclusively on Sprint in the US. By the time it reached Verizon, iPhone had already captured the market.
