# Zano Drone

Europe's most-funded Kickstarter tech project at the time, promising a nano drone that would autonomously follow and film you. Delivered a barely functional product before collapsing in scandal.

## The Rise

**2014-11** — Kickstarter campaign launches; raises £2.3M ($3.4M) from 12,000 backers

**2015-01** — Campaign ends as most-funded European Kickstarter project

**2015-06** — Production delays mount; technical issues with flight stability discovered

## The Fall

**2015-11** — First units shipped to backers; reviewers find them nearly unflyable

**2015-12** — BBC Click investigation reveals major technical failures and misleading marketing

**2016-01** — Torquing Group (Zano's parent) goes into liquidation

## Key Lessons

### Demo videos can be CGI

Zano's marketing showed smooth aerial footage. The actual product couldn't maintain altitude. Backers were sold a dream, not a product.

### Miniaturization is harder than it looks

Packing GPS, camera, stabilization, and battery into a palm-sized drone was technically ambitious. The team couldn't deliver.

### Crowdfunding lacks accountability

When the company folded, backers had no recourse. Kickstarter's terms explicitly state projects may fail.
