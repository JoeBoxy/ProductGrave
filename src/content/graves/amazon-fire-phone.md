# Amazon Fire Phone

Amazon's first and only smartphone, featuring 'Dynamic Perspective' 3D cameras and deep Amazon integration. Priced at iPhone levels but lacking the app ecosystem, it sold poorly and was discontinued within a year.

## The Rise

**2010-01** — Lab126 begins secret phone development under Jeff Bezos' direct oversight

**2014-06** — Announced with Dynamic Perspective and Firefly visual search

**2014-07** — Launches exclusively on AT&T at $199 with contract — same price as iPhone

## The Fall

**2014-08** — Reviews praise hardware but criticize limited app selection and AT&T exclusivity

**2014-09** — Price dropped to $0.99 with contract after poor sales

**2014-10** — Amazon takes $170M write-down on unsold Fire Phone inventory

**2015-08** — Officially discontinued; no successor announced

## Key Lessons

### Ecosystem lock-in is not a consumer value proposition

The Fire Phone was designed to make Amazon purchases easier. Consumers wanted a phone, not a shopping device.

### App gaps are fatal in mobile

No Snapchat, no Instagram, no Google Maps. Without the Google Play Store, the Fire Phone was dead on arrival for most users.

### Carrier exclusivity is suicide

iPhone could pull off AT&T exclusivity in 2007. In 2014, with Android dominating, limiting to one carrier killed any chance of adoption.
