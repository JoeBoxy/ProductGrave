# App.net

A subscription-based social network and API platform pitched as an ad-free, developer-friendly alternative to Twitter. Raised $800K from crowdfunding but couldn't attract enough paying users to sustain itself.

## The Rise

**2012-07** — Dalton Caldwell publishes 'What Twitter Could Have Been' manifesto

**2012-08** — Kickstarter-like crowdfunding campaign launches; raises $800K in 30 days

**2012-10** — Platform opens to developers; microblogging app Alpha launches

## The Fall

**2013-05** — Introduces free tier with limited features to boost adoption

**2013-11** — Admits user growth is far below projections

**2014-05** — Returns remaining funds to backers; places app in maintenance mode

## Key Lessons

### Users don't pay for social networks

The entire history of social media shows users expect free. App.net's $5/month fee was a non-starter for mass adoption.

### Developer platforms need developers

App.net bet on developers building apps on its API. But with few users, developers had no incentive to build.

### Principled stands don't pay the bills

Dalton Caldwell's anti-advertising philosophy was admirable. But Twitter's free, ad-supported model won because it aligned with user expectations.
