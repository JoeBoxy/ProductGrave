# Yahoo Answers

A community-driven Q&A platform where users could ask and answer questions on any topic. At its peak, it was one of the most visited sites on the internet. Became infamous for low-quality answers and memes. Shut down by Verizon-owned Yahoo in 2021.

## The Rise

**2005-06** — Launched as replacement for paid Ask Yahoo service

**2006-01** — Rapid growth; becomes one of Yahoo's most popular properties

**2009-01** — Peak popularity; millions of daily questions and answers

**2011-01** — Competition from Quora and Stack Overflow erodes quality user base

## The Fall

**2014-01** — Becomes more famous for memes and bad answers than useful content

**2017-06** — Verizon acquires Yahoo; Answers seen as low-value legacy property

**2021-04** — Yahoo announces Answers will shut down on May 4, 2021

**2021-05** — Shuts down; 16 years of Q&A history deleted

## Key Lessons

### Unmoderated communities descend into chaos

Yahoo Answers had no quality control. The result was a mix of genuine questions, trolling, and absurdity. Without curation, platforms become unusable.

### Quora proved that identity matters for Q&A

Quora required real names and built reputation systems. The answers were dramatically better. Yahoo Answers' anonymity became its weakness.

### Verizon bought Yahoo to destroy it

Verizon acquired Yahoo for $4.5B and proceeded to kill or sell almost everything: Yahoo Answers, Yahoo Groups, Yahoo Mail became an afterthought. It was an asset strip, not an investment.
