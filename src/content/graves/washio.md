# Washio

An on-demand laundry and dry cleaning delivery service that epitomized the 'Uber for X' boom. Raised $17 million but couldn't make the unit economics work as customer acquisition costs soared.

## The Rise

**2013-01** — Founded in Los Angeles

**2014-01** — Expands to San Francisco, Washington DC, Chicago, and Boston

**2014-09** — Raises $10.5M Series A led by Canaan Partners

## The Fall

**2015-01** — Peak operations in seven cities with free pickup and delivery

**2016-01** — Struggles to raise further funding as 'Uber for X' sector collapses

**2016-08** — Shuts down operations; assets sold to competitor Rinse

## Key Lessons

### Uber for X rarely works outside of transportation

Laundry has thin margins, irregular demand, and high operational complexity. The Uber model doesn't map cleanly to every service category.

### Free delivery is a drug

Washio offered free pickup and delivery to acquire users. When they tried to introduce fees, customers churned immediately.

### Customer acquisition cost kills thin-margin businesses

The cost of acquiring a laundry customer exceeded the lifetime value of that customer. The more they grew, the more they lost.
