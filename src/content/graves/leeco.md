# LeEco

A Chinese conglomerate that tried to build an ecosystem spanning smartphones, TVs, electric cars, content streaming, and cloud services. Raised billions but collapsed under debt and founder Jia Yueting's erratic expansion.

## The Rise

**2004-01** — Founded as LeTV, a Chinese video streaming platform

**2012-01** — Enters smartphone and TV hardware market

**2014-12** — Announces LeSEE electric car project

**2016-04** — US launch event in San Francisco; acquisitions of Vizio and Faraday Future announced

## The Fall

**2016-10** — Cash crisis emerges; suppliers unpaid; stock price plummets

**2017-03** — Vizio acquisition collapses due to regulatory and financial issues

**2017-06** — Jia Yueting flees to US; assets frozen by Chinese courts

**2017-07** — Most operations suspended; massive layoffs across all divisions

## Key Lessons

### Don't try to be Apple without Apple's cash

LeEco tried to replicate Apple's ecosystem strategy but spread itself across too many capital-intensive industries simultaneously.

### Cross-subsidies only work with profitable core businesses

LeEco sold phones and TVs below cost to attract users to its content. But the content business also lost money. Everything subsidized everything else into bankruptcy.

### Founder cults are dangerous

Jia Yueting's grand vision and aggressive expansion went unchecked by the board. When the music stopped, the founder was on a plane to California.
