# Mt. Gox

The world's largest Bitcoin exchange, handling 70% of all global Bitcoin transactions at its peak. Collapsed in 2014 after announcing that 850,000 bitcoins (worth ~$450M at the time) had been stolen or lost due to a security flaw.

## The Rise

**2010-07** — Founded by Jed McCaleb as a Magic: The Gathering card exchange, pivots to Bitcoin

**2011-03** — Sold to Mark Karpelès; becomes dominant Bitcoin exchange

**2013-04** — Handles 70% of all Bitcoin transactions globally

**2013-11** — First suspicions of insolvency emerge; withdrawals delayed

## The Fall

**2014-02** — Halts all withdrawals; files for bankruptcy in Tokyo

**2014-02** — Announces 850,000 bitcoins (~$450M) are missing

**2014-04** — Later finds 200,000 bitcoins in 'forgotten' wallet

**2021-10** — Rehabilitation plan approved; creditors may finally recover some funds

## Key Lessons

### Don't store customer funds in a hot wallet

Mt. Gox kept most customer Bitcoin in online 'hot wallets' vulnerable to hacking. Basic security hygiene would have prevented the catastrophe.

### Centralized exchanges are single points of failure

Mt. Gox proved that centralized crypto exchanges can fail catastrophically. 'Not your keys, not your coins' became the mantra of the crypto community.

### Japan's regulatory framework was too slow

Mt. Gox operated without meaningful oversight until after the collapse. Japan later created the world's first crypto exchange licensing regime.
