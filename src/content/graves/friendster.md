# Friendster

The original social networking pioneer with 3 million users in its first year. Died from chronic technical failures, executive turnover, and refusal to sell to Google for $30 million.

## The Rise

**2002-03** — Launched by Jonathan Abrams

**2002-08** — Hits 3 million registered users

**2003-01** — Turns down Google acquisition offer of $30 million

**2003-04** — Raises VC from Kleiner Perkins and Benchmark Capital

## The Fall

**2004-04** — MySpace overtakes Friendster in page views

**2006-08** — Adds widgets and fan profiles — four years after MySpace

**2009-12** — Sold to MOL Global for $26.4 million

**2011-06** — Relaunches as social gaming site; all social profiles deleted

**2015-06** — Shuts down permanently

## Key Lessons

### Performance is a feature

Friendster pages took up to 40 seconds to load. Instead of fixing infrastructure, executives added more features that slowed things down.

### Know when to sell

Turning down Google's $30M offer in 2003 was hubris. Two years later, the company was worth a fraction of that.

### Executive consistency matters

Six CEOs in seven years. Every new leader changed direction, jerking engineers and product strategy around.
