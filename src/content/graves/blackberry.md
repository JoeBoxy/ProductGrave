# BlackBerry

The Canadian company that created the smartphone category with its secure email and physical keyboards. Died because it couldn't give up its keyboard and enterprise focus while consumers moved to touchscreen iPhones.

## The Rise

**1999-01** — BlackBerry 850 launched with wireless email

**2003-01** — BlackBerry goes mainstream with color screens and phone functionality

**2007-06** — iPhone launches; BlackBerry CEO calls it 'not a threat'

## The Fall

**2008-11** — Storm touchscreen phone launches to terrible reviews

**2013-01** — BlackBerry 10 OS and Z10 phone finally launch — years too late

**2013-09** — Announces plans to go private after $965M quarterly loss

**2022-01** — Stops supporting BlackBerry OS devices entirely

## Key Lessons

### Don't mock disruption from below

BlackBerry executives publicly dismissed the iPhone. By the time they took it seriously, it was too late.

### Enterprise focus can blind you to consumer shifts

BlackBerry prioritized security and IT department approval while consumers wanted apps, cameras, and touchscreens.

### Platforms are winner-take-most

iOS and Android built app ecosystems that became impossible to compete with. No apps = no users = no developers.
