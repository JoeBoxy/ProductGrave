# StumbleUpon

A discovery engine that randomly sent users to websites matching their interests. Once had 25 million users but failed to evolve as social media and algorithmic feeds took over content discovery.

## The Rise

**2001-11** — Founded at University of Calgary by Garrett Camp and friends

**2007-05** — Acquired by eBay for $75 million

**2009-04** — Founders buy StumbleUpon back from eBay with investors

## The Fall

**2011-01** — Hits 25 million registered users

**2012-01** — Garrett Camp leaves to focus on Uber (which he also co-founded)

**2013-01** — Traffic declines as Pinterest, Reddit, and Facebook capture discovery

**2018-06** — Shuts down; transitions to Mix.com which also fails

## Key Lessons

### Randomness is not a sustainable product strategy

StumbleUpon's core mechanic was serendipity. But as users' time became more valuable, algorithmic curation (Pinterest, TikTok) beat random discovery.

### Founder attention matters

Garrett Camp co-founded both StumbleUpon and Uber. When he focused on Uber, StumbleUpon withered. Founders can't split attention between two high-growth companies.

### eBay is where good products go to die

eBay acquired StumbleUpon, Skype, and others — then failed to integrate any of them. The acquisition was a tax write-off, not a strategy.
