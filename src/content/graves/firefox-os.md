# Firefox OS

Mozilla's attempt to create a mobile operating system based entirely on web technologies (HTML5, CSS, JavaScript). Aimed at emerging markets but couldn't compete with Android's ecosystem and hardware diversity.

## The Rise

**2011-07** — Announced as 'Boot to Gecko' project at Mobile World Congress

**2012-02** — Renamed Firefox OS; first developer phones announced

**2013-02** — First consumer devices launch in Spain with Telefónica

## The Fall

**2013-07** — ZTE and Alcatel announce Firefox OS phones for emerging markets

**2014-01** — Launches in 14 countries across South America, Europe, and Asia

**2014-12** — Sales disappointing; Mozilla pivots focus to connected devices

**2015-12** — Officially discontinued; Mozilla shifts to IoT efforts

## Key Lessons

### Web technologies weren't ready for mobile OS

HTML5 apps in 2013 were slow and limited compared to native apps. Users noticed the performance gap immediately.

### The low-end phone market belongs to Android

Firefox OS targeted $25 smartphones in emerging markets. But Android Go and cheap Chinese Android phones offered better experiences at the same price.

### Open source without ecosystem is just code

Mozilla had noble intentions but no app developers, no major carrier support, and no hardware partners with real brand power.
