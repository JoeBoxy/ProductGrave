# Ubuntu Phone

Canonical's attempt to bring Ubuntu Linux to mobile devices with a 'convergence' vision — one device that powers your phone and desktop. The Ubuntu Edge crowdfunding campaign raised $12.8M but fell short of its $32M goal.

## The Rise

**2013-01** — Announced at CES; convergence vision revealed

**2013-08** — Ubuntu Edge Indiegogo campaign launches with $32M goal

**2013-08** — Campaign raises $12.8M — most-funded crowdfunding campaign at the time, but still fails

## The Fall

**2014-02** — First Ubuntu Touch devices from BQ and Meizu announced

**2015-01** — Limited release in Europe; no carrier partnerships in major markets

**2017-04** — Canonical officially ends Ubuntu Phone investment; community takes over

## Key Lessons

### Convergence is a solution looking for a problem

The idea of one device for phone and desktop sounded compelling. But in practice, nobody wanted to dock their phone. Laptops and phones served different use cases.

### Crowdfunding failure is market signal

The Ubuntu Edge campaign was the most ambitious hardware crowdfunding project ever. Its failure proved there wasn't enough demand for an Ubuntu phone.

### Desktop Linux popularity doesn't translate to mobile

Ubuntu was the most popular Linux desktop. But mobile is a completely different market requiring carrier relationships, app ecosystems, and hardware partnerships.
