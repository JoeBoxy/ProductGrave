# Posterous

A lightweight blogging platform that let users publish posts by simply sending an email. Acquired by Twitter in 2012 and shut down in 2013 as Twitter narrowed its focus to short-form content.

## The Rise

**2008-06** — Founded by Y Combinator alums Sachin Agarwal and Garry Tan

**2008-07** — Launches with email-to-blog as core feature

**2009-01** — Gains traction as simpler alternative to WordPress and Tumblr

## The Fall

**2010-09** — Adds Spaces for group blogging; competes with Tumblr

**2012-03** — Twitter acquires Posterous for reported $10-15M

**2012-09** — Twitter announces Posterous will shut down

**2013-04** — All Posterous sites taken offline; users given export tools

## Key Lessons

### Being simpler than competitors is not enough

Posterous was easier than WordPress but lacked customization. Tumblr offered simplicity AND personality. Email-to-blog was a feature, not a platform.

### Acqui-hires often kill products

Twitter bought Posterous primarily for its engineering team. The product was always going to die — it was a talent acquisition disguised as a product acquisition.

### Platform shutdowns destroy trust

When Posterous shut down, users lost years of content. The lesson: don't build your online presence on platforms that can disappear overnight.
