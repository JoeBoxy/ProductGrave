# Anki

A robotics and AI company that created consumer robots like Cozmo and Vector, small AI-powered toys with personality. Despite critical acclaim and strong sales, the company burned through $182M and abruptly shut down in 2019.

## The Rise

**2010-01** — Founded by three Carnegie Mellon robotics PhDs

**2013-01** — Raises $50M Series A from Andreessen Horowitz and Index Ventures

**2016-10** — Cozmo launches; becomes bestselling toy on Amazon

## The Fall

**2017-01** — Raises $77M Series C; total funding reaches $182M

**2018-08** — Vector launches with Alexa integration; praised by reviewers

**2019-04** — Abruptly shuts down; all staff laid off without warning

**2019-12** — Digital Dream Labs acquires Vector IP and revives the product

## Key Lessons

### $182M is too much money for a toy company

Anki raised VC-level funding but sold consumer toys. The expectations were startup-scale, but the margins were toy-industry thin.

### Robotics hardware is brutally expensive

Every Cozmo and Vector required precision manufacturing, custom chips, and complex software. The BOM (bill of materials) left almost no margin.

### Abrupt shutdowns destroy customer trust

Anki shut down overnight. Vector owners suddenly had a $250 paperweight. The company had no transition plan for its most loyal customers.
