# Munchery

A meal delivery service that prepared chef-crafted meals in central kitchens and delivered them to customers. Raised $125M but shut down abruptly in 2019 after failing to achieve unit economics or find a buyer.

## The Rise

**2010-01** — Founded by Tri Tran and Conrad Chu in San Francisco

**2014-01** — Expands to Seattle and Los Angeles

**2015-01** — Raises $85M Series C; total funding reaches $125M

## The Fall

**2016-01** — Launches 'Munchery 2.0' with on-demand delivery model

**2017-01** — Cuts 30% of staff; shifts from employee drivers to gig workers

**2018-01** — Closes LA and Seattle operations; focuses on San Francisco

**2019-01** — Abruptly shuts down; customers with gift cards left empty-handed

## Key Lessons

### Prepared meal delivery has terrible unit economics

Munchery had the costs of a restaurant (kitchens, chefs, ingredients) plus the costs of delivery. Each order lost money. The math never worked.

### Pivoting to gig workers destroys quality control

When Munchery switched from employee drivers to gig workers, food arrived cold and damaged. The quality advantage that justified premium pricing vanished.

### Don't take gift card money if you're about to die

Munchery sold gift cards right up until shutdown, leaving customers with worthless balances. The optics were terrible and may have violated consumer protection laws.
