# Magic Leap

An augmented reality company that raised $3.5 billion based on breathtaking demo videos and Silicon Valley hype. When the actual product launched, it was underwhelming, overpriced, and years behind schedule.

## The Rise

**2010-01** — Founded by Rony Abovitz

**2014-10** — Raises $542M led by Google; valuation soars

**2016-12** — Reveals first teaser video of whale jumping out of gym floor

## The Fall

**2018-08** — Raises $502M from Saudi Arabia's sovereign wealth fund

**2018-08** — Magic Leap One headset launches at $2,295; reviews are mediocre

**2020-05** — CEO Rony Abovitz steps down; pivot to enterprise AR announced

**2024-10** — Most of remaining staff laid off; assets sold to an unnamed buyer

## Key Lessons

### Demo videos are not products

Magic Leap's famous whale video was allegedly CGI, not actual headset footage. The gap between promise and reality destroyed credibility.

### Enterprise pivots rarely save consumer failures

When the consumer headset flopped, Magic Leap pivoted to enterprise. But enterprises had already moved on to Microsoft HoloLens and other proven solutions.

### Hype has a half-life

$3.5B bought Magic Leap a decade of runway. But no amount of funding can replace actual product-market fit.
