# Inbox by Gmail

A radical reimagining of Gmail that automatically categorized emails, highlighted key information, and introduced features like 'Snooze' and 'Bundles.' Loved by power users but killed by Google to consolidate around standard Gmail.

## The Rise

**2014-10** — Launched as invite-only experiment alongside Gmail

**2015-05** — Opens to all users; praised for innovative email organization

## The Fall

**2016-01** — Introduces 'Smart Reply' AI-generated responses

**2018-09** — Google announces Inbox will shut down in March 2019

**2019-04** — Officially shut down; some features ported to Gmail but core UX lost

## Key Lessons

### Google kills its best products to save its mediocre ones

Inbox was widely considered superior to Gmail. But maintaining two email products was inefficient, so Google killed the better one.

### Power users have no leverage

Inbox's most devoted users petitioned, tweeted, and begged Google to keep it. Google ignored them. When you don't pay for the product, your opinion doesn't matter.

### Experimentation without commitment is wasteful

Google spent five years developing Inbox, acquired millions of loyal users, then threw it away. The pattern discourages user investment in Google products.
