# Modu

An Israeli startup that created a tiny modular phone core that could be inserted into various 'jackets' to transform functionality. Founded by the inventor of the USB flash drive. Raised $120M but failed as smartphones made dedicated device categories obsolete.

## The Rise

**2007-01** — Founded by Dov Moran, inventor of USB flash drive

**2008-02** — Launches Modu 1; Guinness World Records names it smallest phone

**2009-01** — Partnerships with carriers in Israel, UK, and Russia

## The Fall

**2009-09** — Modu T (touchscreen version) launches to mediocre reviews

**2010-01** — iPhone and Android destroy the 'feature phone' market

**2010-06** — Google acquires Modu patents for $4.9M (primarily for Android IP)

**2011-02** — Company officially liquidated

## Key Lessons

### Modularity is a solution looking for a problem

Modu's jackets were clever but unnecessary. By 2010, smartphones did everything jackets did — camera, music, GPS — in one device.

### $120M is a lot for a feature phone in the smartphone era

Modu raised massive funding but was building feature phones when the world wanted iPhones. The timing was catastrophic.

### Even legendary founders can miss the paradigm shift

Dov Moran invented the USB drive. But he didn't see that smartphones would swallow all other consumer electronics. Past success doesn't guarantee future insight.
