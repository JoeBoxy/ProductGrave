# Skully

A smart motorcycle helmet featuring a rear-view camera and heads-up display. Raised $2.4M on Indiegogo and $15M total but went bankrupt in 2016. Investigations revealed the Weller brothers had misused company funds for personal expenses.

## The Rise

**2013-01** — Founded by Marcus Weller after a motorcycle accident inspires the concept

**2014-08** — Indiegogo campaign raises $2.4M

**2015-01** — Shows working prototype at CES; wins innovation awards

## The Fall

**2015-06** — Raises $11M Series A from East Bay Ventures and others

**2016-01** — Manufacturing delays; founders accused of using funds for personal luxury purchases

**2016-07** — Company files for Chapter 7 bankruptcy

**2016-08** — Lawsuit reveals founders spent company money on sports cars, apartments, and strip clubs

## Key Lessons

### Founder fraud is devastating in hardware

Unlike software, hardware startups have real manufacturing costs. When founders steal from a hardware company, there's no product to show for the investment.

### Motorcycle helmets are safety-critical and hard to certify

Skully never obtained DOT or ECE safety certifications. A 'smart' helmet that isn't certified is legally a toy, not safety equipment.

### Crowdfunding success doesn't validate the business

$2.4M from excited motorcyclists looked like validation. But it was pre-orders, not proof of manufacturing capability or market demand at scale.
