# ChaCha

A human-powered search engine where real people answered user questions via SMS and web. Raised $96M but couldn't survive the shift to smartphones and AI-powered assistants like Siri and Google.

## The Rise

**2005-09** — Founded by Scott Jones (inventor of voicemail) and Brad Bostic

**2006-01** — Launches SMS-based Q&A service

**2008-01** — Expands to web; claims to answer 1 million questions daily

## The Fall

**2011-01** — iPhone app launches but struggles against Siri and Google voice search

**2014-01** — Traffic declines sharply as AI assistants improve

**2016-11** — Shuts down operations; lays off all staff

**2016-12** — Domain and assets sold; company ceases to exist

## Key Lessons

### Human-powered scale is expensive

ChaCha paid 'guides' to answer questions. At $0.10-$0.20 per answer, margins were razor-thin and quality was inconsistent.

### Technology obsolescence happens fast

In 2006, human search was innovative. By 2011, Siri and Google Voice Search provided instant answers for free. ChaCha's model became obsolete almost overnight.

### SMS-based services died with the smartphone

ChaCha's core mechanic was texting questions. When smartphones put the full internet in everyone's pocket, SMS Q&A felt quaint and slow.
