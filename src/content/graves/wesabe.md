# Wesabe

A personal finance management tool that helped users track spending and achieve financial goals. Launched before Mint but lost the market due to inferior UX, slower bank connections, and Mint's superior design and marketing.

## The Rise

**2005-11** — Founded by Jason Knight and Marc Hedlund

**2006-11** — Beta launch at TechCrunch 40 conference

**2007-09** — Mint launches with superior design and faster setup

## The Fall

**2008-01** — Wesabe requires manual data entry; Mint offers automatic bank sync

**2009-09** — Intuit acquires Mint for $170 million

**2010-06** — Wesabe shuts down; founder writes famous post-mortem analyzing why Mint won

## Key Lessons

### UX beats philosophy

Wesabe believed users should manually enter transactions to build financial awareness. Mint auto-synced banks and looked beautiful. Users chose convenience over virtue.

### Being first doesn't matter if you're not best

Wesabe launched a year before Mint. But Mint solved the onboarding problem in 5 minutes while Wesabe took hours. First-mover advantage is a myth.

### The post-mortem is as valuable as the product

Wesabe's founder wrote a brutally honest analysis of why they lost. It's become required reading for startup founders — a dignified end for a failed product.
