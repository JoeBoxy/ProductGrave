# Napster

The peer-to-peer file sharing service that revolutionized how people consumed music — and nearly destroyed the recording industry. Shut down by court order after lawsuits from Metallica and the RIAA.

## The Rise

**1999-06** — Created by 18-year-old Shawn Fanning while at Northeastern University

**1999-12** — Hits 10 million users; 80 million songs shared

**2000-04** — Metallica files copyright infringement lawsuit

## The Fall

**2000-07** — Federal judge orders Napster to remove all copyrighted material

**2000-10** — RIAA files additional lawsuit seeking $100,000 per song shared

**2001-03** — Ninth Circuit Court of Appeals rules Napster must remain shut down

**2001-07** — Shuts down file-sharing service after settlement

## Key Lessons

### Disrupting incumbents without legal cover is dangerous

Napster's technology was brilliant but legally indefensible. It had no licensing deals and no plan for when the lawsuits came.

### Pioneers often pave the way for others

Napster died, but its legacy lives on in Spotify, Apple Music, and legal streaming. It proved people would pay for convenience if the price was right.

### Teenage founders need adult guidance

Shawn Fanning was 18. Sean Parker was 19. Their naivety about legal and business strategy made them easy targets for the music industry.
