# Beme

A social media app founded by YouTube star Casey Neistat that captured 4-second video clips without letting users preview or edit them. Acquired by CNN for a reported $25M but shut down just four months later as CNN pivoted to its own digital video strategy.

## The Rise

**2014-07** — Founded by Casey Neistat and Matt Hackett

**2015-07** — iOS app launches; gains 1.1 million downloads in first week

**2015-08** — Android app launches

## The Fall

**2016-05** — CNN acquires Beme for reported $25 million

**2016-11** — CNN shuts down Beme app entirely

**2017-01** — CNN announces Beme team will build a new digital news platform

**2018-01** — The new platform never materializes; team disperses

## Key Lessons

### Inauthenticity can't be fixed by authenticity features

Beme's no-preview gimmick was supposed to create 'authentic' content. But users didn't want uneditable videos — they wanted to look good on social media.

### Celebrity founders attract media, not product-market fit

Casey Neistat's YouTube fame drove initial downloads. But his audience didn't stick around. Fame is not a moat.

### Media companies shouldn't buy apps

CNN bought Beme for its 'digital DNA' but had no idea what to do with it. Four months later they killed it. Media acquisitions of tech startups rarely work.
