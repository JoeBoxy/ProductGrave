# iTunes Ping

A music-oriented social network built into iTunes 10 that let users follow artists and friends. Announced by Steve Jobs himself but abandoned within two years due to spam, lack of features, and Apple's general inability to do social.

## The Rise

**2010-09** — Announced by Steve Jobs at Apple fall music event

**2010-09** — Launches with 160 million iTunes users as potential audience

**2010-10** — Spam floods the platform within weeks

## The Fall

**2011-01** — Facebook integration announced but quickly scrapped due to 'onerous terms'

**2012-06** — Ping barely mentioned at WWDC; Apple shifts focus to Facebook/Twitter integration

**2012-09** — Removed in iTunes 11; Apple executive calls it 'one of Apple's few mistakes'

## Key Lessons

### Social features need a social DNA

Apple is brilliant at hardware and software but culturally incapable of building social products. Ping, like Game Center, felt bolted-on and awkward.

### Spam kills social platforms fast

Ping was overrun by fake accounts and spam within weeks. Apple had no content moderation infrastructure — a fatal oversight for social.

### Even Steve Jobs couldn't will social into existence

Jobs announced Ping with typical confidence. But Apple's closed, controlled culture is antithetical to the openness social networks require.
