# Google Nexus Q

Google's first attempt at a living room media streamer — a bizarre spherical device made in the USA with a $299 price tag. Pulled from the market after months of terrible reviews and consumer confusion.

## The Rise

**2012-05** — Unveiled at Google I/O alongside Nexus 7 tablet

**2012-06** — Launches at $299; requires Android phone as remote control

**2012-07** — Reviews universally negative; critics call it 'useless' and 'overpriced'

## The Fall

**2012-08** — Google halts consumer sales; gives free units to I/O attendees instead

**2013-01** — Officially discontinued; no restock or successor

**2013-07** — Chromecast launches at $35 — the product Nexus Q should have been

## Key Lessons

### Made in USA is not a consumer selling point

Nexus Q's $299 price was partly due to US manufacturing. Consumers didn't care where it was made — they cared what it did.

### Requiring a phone as a remote is user-hostile

Nexus Q had no remote control. You needed an Android phone to operate it. This limited the audience to Android owners willing to use their phone as a TV remote.

### Kill your darlings quickly

Google pulled Nexus Q within months and pivoted to Chromecast. The $35 Chromecast became a massive success. Admitting failure fast saved Google from a bigger mistake.
