# Google Latitude

A location-sharing service that let users share their real-time location with friends and family. Integrated into Google Maps but shut down in 2013 as Google tried to consolidate social features into Google+.

## The Rise

**2008-05** — Announced at Google I/O as 'My Location' sharing feature

**2009-02** — Launches as standalone Google Latitude

**2010-01** — 5 million active users; integrated into Google Maps mobile

**2011-01** — Location history feature added

## The Fall

**2012-01** — Google+ launches; Latitude's social features seen as redundant

**2013-07** — Google announces Latitude shutdown

**2013-08** — Shut down; location sharing moved to Google+

**2015-01** — Google+ location sharing also killed; concept abandoned

## Key Lessons

### Location sharing is inherently creepy

Latitude struggled to find mass adoption because most people don't want to be tracked 24/7. The use case (parents tracking kids) was too narrow.

### Google+ destroyed more products than it saved

Latitude, Reader, and Buzz were all sacrificed on the altar of Google+. Google+ failed anyway, making these deaths even more pointless.

### Apple and Google both failed at social

Latitude was Google's attempt at social location sharing. Apple's Find My Friends did no better. This space belongs to Snap Maps and WhatsApp live location.
