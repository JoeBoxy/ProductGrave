# eToys

An online toy retailer that became a dot-com darling with a market cap exceeding Toys 'R' Us at its peak. Burned through $400M in funding and went bankrupt when the bubble burst.

## The Rise

**1997-06** — Founded by former Disney executive Toby Lenk

**1999-05** — IPO raises $164M; stock price jumps 300% on first day

**1999-10** — Market cap reaches $10 billion — higher than Toys 'R' Us

## The Fall

**1999-12** — Holiday season disaster; warehouse can't fulfill orders; customer service overwhelmed

**2000-01** — Stock crashes 70% after disappointing holiday results

**2001-02** — Files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy; assets liquidated

## Key Lessons

### Market cap is not business value

eToys was worth more than Toys 'R' Us on paper despite having no profits and a fraction of the revenue. The market was irrationally exuberant.

### Logistics beat marketing

eToys spent millions on Super Bowl ads but couldn't deliver toys by Christmas. Operations matter more than brand awareness.

### Customer service scales with volume

During the 1999 holiday rush, customers waited hours on hold and never received orders. One bad holiday killed the brand.
