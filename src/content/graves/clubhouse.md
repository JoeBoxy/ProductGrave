# Clubhouse

An audio-only social network that exploded during the pandemic with invite-only exclusivity and celebrity users. Valued at $4 billion before crashing as Twitter Spaces, Spotify, and others copied the format.

## The Rise

**2020-03** — Launched quietly during COVID-19 lockdowns

**2021-01** — Viral explosion; invite codes sell for $400 on eBay

**2021-01** — Elon Musk appears on Clubhouse; app crashes from traffic

**2021-04** — Series B at $4 billion valuation led by Andreessen Horowitz

## The Fall

**2021-06** — Twitter launches Spaces; Facebook and Spotify follow with clones

**2021-07** — Downloads plummet 70% as exclusivity fades

**2023-04** — Lays off 50% of staff

**2023-12** — Effectively dead; founders moved to new projects

## Key Lessons

### Scarcity marketing creates temporary hype, not loyalty

Invite-only worked until it didn't. When Clubhouse opened to everyone, the mystique evaporated and users moved on.

### If you can be copied in 90 days, you have no moat

Twitter Spaces replicated Clubhouse's core feature in months. Without proprietary technology or network effects, Clubhouse was defenseless.

### Pandemic-driven growth is often artificial

People were stuck at home and craved human connection. As the world reopened, audio chat rooms lost their appeal.
