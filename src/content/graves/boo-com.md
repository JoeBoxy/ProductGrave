# Boo.com

A would-be global sports fashion e-tailer that burned through $135 million in funding, launched in 18 countries simultaneously, and collapsed within two years due to poor management, slow website, and over-ambition.

## The Rise

**1998-01** — Founded by Swedish entrepreneurs Malmsten, Leander, and Hedelin in London

**1999-05** — Raises initial $9M from investors including JP Morgan and LVMH

**1999-11** — Launches after six-month delay due to technology problems

## The Fall

**1999-11** — First day: 50,000 visitors but only 0.25% conversion rate; site takes 81 minutes to check out

**2000-02** — Conversion rate improves to 3% after site relaunch, but burn rate remains catastrophic

**2000-05** — Investors refuse further funding; Boo.com goes into liquidation

## Key Lessons

### Start small, then expand

Boo tried to launch in 18 countries across two continents simultaneously. Not even Amazon did that.

### User experience beats flashy tech

The 3D product viewer and virtual assistant 'Miss Boo' were gimmicks. Users just wanted to buy shoes quickly.

### Hire financial management early

Boo took six months to hire a CFO and had no COO. $135M burned with no adult supervision.
