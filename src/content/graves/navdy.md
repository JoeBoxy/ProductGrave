# Navdy

A heads-up display (HUD) device that projected smartphone navigation and notifications onto a transparent screen mounted on car windshields. Raised $6.5M on Kickstarter and pre-orders but struggled with manufacturing and eventually went bankrupt.

## The Rise

**2013-01** — Founded by Doug Simpson and Adam Grabell

**2014-08** — Pre-order campaign raises $1.3M

**2015-10** — Kickstarter raises $6.5M from 10,000+ backers

## The Fall

**2016-01** — First shipping delays announced; manufacturing problems cited

**2016-12** — Navdy begins shipping to some backers but quality issues emerge

**2017-06** — Price drops from $499 to $299 to clear inventory

**2017-12** — Company files for bankruptcy; most backers never receive units

## Key Lessons

### Car tech is heavily regulated and hard to certify

Navdy had to pass automotive safety standards in multiple countries. The certification process was longer and more expensive than anticipated.

### HUDs solve a problem phones already solved

By the time Navdy shipped, every car had a phone mount and every phone had Google Maps. Navdy offered marginal improvement at 10x the cost.

### Kickstarter hardware has a terrible track record

Navdy is one of many hardware Kickstarter campaigns that raised millions and delivered little. The platform attracts optimists who underestimate manufacturing complexity.
