# Turntable.fm

A social music platform where users could create virtual rooms, take turns DJing, and vote on songs. Became a cult hit among music fans before licensing issues and competition from Spotify forced its closure.

## The Rise

**2011-01** — Founded by Billy Chasen and Seth Goldstein

**2011-06** — Launches in beta; invite-only system creates buzz

**2011-09** — Hits 140,000 users; celebrities including Kanye West and Lady Gaga join

## The Fall

**2012-01** — Raises $7M Series A from Union Square Ventures

**2012-06** — US licensing deals restrict international expansion

**2012-12** — Traffic declines as Spotify and Pandora grow

**2013-12** — Shuts down; pivots team to live-event app

## Key Lessons

### Music licensing is a moat and a graveyard

Turntable.fm needed deals with all major labels. The negotiations consumed resources and limited features. Without them, it was legally exposed.

### Social features need sustainable engagement loops

DJing was fun but exhausting. Most users became passive listeners. When engagement dropped, the social magic faded.

### Celebrity users don't build sustainable businesses

Kanye and Lady Gaga brought press but not retention. The core experience needed to stand on its own.
