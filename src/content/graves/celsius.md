# Celsius Network

A cryptocurrency lending platform that promised extraordinarily high yields on crypto deposits. Raised nearly $900M but collapsed in 2022 when it froze all withdrawals and filed for bankruptcy, leaving 1.7 million customers unable to access their funds.

## The Rise

**2017-01** — Founded by Alex Mashinsky, former VoIP pioneer

**2018-06** — Launches Celsius app offering interest on crypto deposits

**2021-01** — Claims $3B in assets under management and 800K users

**2021-10** — Raises $400M at $3B valuation

## The Fall

**2022-05** — LUNA/UST collapse causes massive losses in Celsius's portfolio

**2022-06** — Freezes all customer withdrawals citing 'extreme market conditions'

**2022-07** — Files for Chapter 11 bankruptcy

**2023-07** — Mashinsky arrested and charged with fraud by DOJ and SEC

## Key Lessons

### If yield seems too good to be true, it's a Ponzi

Celsius offered 17% APY while traditional banks offered 0.5%. The math never worked — they were taking reckless risks to generate returns.

### Founder credibility doesn't guarantee integrity

Alex Mashinsky was a respected telecom entrepreneur. But he repeatedly lied about Celsius's risk exposure while secretly withdrawing his own funds.

### Crypto lending needs regulation, not just trust

Celsius operated like a bank but without any of the safeguards. Customer deposits were uninsured and the company made reckless bets with them.
