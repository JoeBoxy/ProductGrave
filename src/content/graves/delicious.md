# Delicious

The pioneering social bookmarking service that introduced tagging to the web. Bought by Yahoo in 2005, neglected for years, then sold repeatedly before finally shutting down in 2017.

## The Rise

**2003-09** — Launched by Joshua Schachter as a personal project

**2005-12** — Acquired by Yahoo for a reported $15-30 million

**2008-01** — Peak popularity; 5.3 million users and 180 million bookmarks

## The Fall

**2010-12** — Yahoo announces 'sunset' of Delicious; user backlash forces reversal

**2011-04** — Sold by Yahoo to AVOS Systems (YouTube founders Chad Hurley and Steve Chen)

**2014-05** — AVOS sells Delicious to Science Inc.

**2017-06** — Acquired by Pinboard; reading-only mode announced; effectively dead

## Key Lessons

### Acquisitions by big tech often mean death by neglect

Yahoo bought Delicious and then did nothing with it for six years. The founders left, innovation stopped, and users migrated to Pinboard and browser bookmarks.

### Tagging was revolutionary but not defensible

Delicious invented social tagging, but the feature was quickly copied by Flickr, YouTube, and eventually every platform. Without network effects, the moat vanished.

### Being sold three times is a bad sign

Each owner had less interest in the product than the last. By the time Pinboard bought it, Delicious was a ghost ship with no active users.
