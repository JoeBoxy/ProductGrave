# Sprig

An on-demand meal delivery startup promising healthy meals delivered in 15 minutes or less. Raised $57M from top investors but shut down in 2017 when it became clear the economics couldn't work against established food delivery apps.

## The Rise

**2013-01** — Founded by Gagan Biyani, former Udemy co-founder

**2014-01** — Launches in San Francisco with 15-minute delivery promise

**2015-01** — Raises $45M Series B from Social Capital and others

## The Fall

**2015-06** — Expands to Chicago and Los Angeles

**2016-01** — Delivery times slip to 30+ minutes; quality complaints rise

**2016-06** — Pivots to 'family-style' meals to improve margins

**2017-05** — Shuts down operations; assets sold to competitor DoorDash

## Key Lessons

### The 'Uber for food' model was always flawed

Sprig, Munchery, and dozens of others tried to combine cooking and delivery. The complexity was too high. Restaurants exist for a reason.

### 15-minute delivery requires massive fixed costs

To deliver in 15 minutes, Sprig needed kitchens in every neighborhood. The real estate and labor costs crushed the business before it scaled.

### When DoorDash is cheaper, you lose

Sprig's meals cost $12-15 with delivery. DoorDash delivered Chipotle for $8. Consumers chose convenience and price over 'healthy' branding.
