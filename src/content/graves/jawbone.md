# Jawbone

A consumer electronics company that pivoted from Bluetooth headsets to speakers to fitness trackers. Raised nearly $1 billion in funding but never achieved profitability, eventually liquidating in 2017.

## The Rise

**1999-01** — Founded as AliphCom, making noise-canceling headsets for the military

**2010-01** — Launches Jambox Bluetooth speaker; becomes design icon

**2011-11** — Enters wearables with UP fitness tracker band

## The Fall

**2014-02** — Valued at $3.3 billion after massive funding rounds

**2015-11** — UP3 tracker launch delayed; quality issues plague products

**2016-01** — Sells remaining inventory to reseller; stops manufacturing

**2017-07** — Begins liquidation process; intellectual property sold off

## Key Lessons

### Pivoting too often confuses the market

Jawbone was a headset company, then a speaker company, then a fitness tracker company. Never mastered any single category.

### Design without reliability is worthless

Jawbone products were beautiful but notoriously unreliable. The UP band had a 40% return rate.

### Raising money is not success

Nearly $1 billion in funding with zero profitability. Jawbone proved that capital can paper over fundamental business model problems for only so long.
