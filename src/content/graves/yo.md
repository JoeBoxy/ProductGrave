# Yo

A minimalist messaging app that did exactly one thing: send the word 'Yo' to friends. Became an overnight viral sensation, raised $1.5 million, then faded into obscurity as quickly as it arrived.

## The Rise

**2014-04** — Launched by Or Arbel in eight hours

**2014-06** — Viral explosion; hits #1 on App Store

**2014-06** — Raises $1.5M at a $10M valuation from investors including Betaworks

## The Fall

**2014-08** — Hacked by college students; exposes security flaws

**2015-01** — Attempts to pivot into 'Yo for IoT' and business notifications

**2016-06** — App removed from App Store; effectively dead

## Key Lessons

### Viral ≠ valuable

Millions of downloads and global press coverage couldn't create lasting value. Users downloaded it as a joke, then deleted it.

### Single-feature apps are features, not products

Yo should have been a feature inside WhatsApp or Messenger. As a standalone app, it had no reason to exist long-term.

### Silicon Valley can fund anything

The fact that Yo raised $1.5M became a symbol of the 2014 app bubble. It proved that hype could outrun common sense.
