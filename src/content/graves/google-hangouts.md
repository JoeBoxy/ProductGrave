# Google Hangouts

Google's attempt to unify its fragmented messaging services into one platform. Despite deep Gmail integration, it was gradually replaced by Google Chat, Meet, and a confusing array of competing products.

## The Rise

**2011-06** — Google+ launches with Hangouts video chat as a key feature

**2013-05** — Standalone Hangouts app launched; merges Google Talk, Google+ Messenger, and Hangouts

**2014-01** — SMS integration added to Android app

## The Fall

**2016-01** — Google announces Hangouts will split into 'Meet' and 'Chat' for enterprise

**2018-12** — Consumer version of Hangouts officially deprecated

**2021-04** — Google Chat becomes default replacement for Hangouts in Gmail

**2022-11** — Hangouts finally shut down for all users

## Key Lessons

### Google's messaging strategy is cursed

Google has killed or fragmented Google Talk, Hangouts, Allo, Duo, Meet, Chat, and Messages. No company has failed at messaging more consistently.

### Forced unification alienates existing users

Hangouts merged multiple services with different user bases. Google+ refugees, Gmail users, and SMS users all had different needs. Nobody was happy.

### Enterprise pivots kill consumer products

When Google decided Hangouts should be an enterprise tool (Meet/Chat), the consumer version was starved of resources. Users migrated to WhatsApp and iMessage.
