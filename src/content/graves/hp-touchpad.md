# HP TouchPad

HP's webOS tablet that launched in July 2011 and was discontinued just 48 days later. The $99 fire sale made it an instant cult classic, but symbolized HP's catastrophic mismanagement of its Palm acquisition.

## The Rise

**2010-04** — HP acquires Palm for $1.2B, primarily for webOS

**2011-02** — TouchPad unveiled at webOS event alongside Pre 3 phone

**2011-07** — Launches at $499; reviews criticize thickness, app shortage, and performance

## The Fall

**2011-08** — HP announces it will discontinue all webOS hardware — 48 days after launch

**2011-08** — Fire sale drops TouchPad to $99; sells out instantly

**2011-12** — webOS open-sourced; HP keeps patents

## Key Lessons

### 48 days is not a product cycle

HP didn't give TouchPad time to improve. The decision to kill it was corporate strategy, not product performance.

### Buying a company for its OS then killing the OS is madness

HP paid $1.2B for Palm/webOS, made two devices, then abandoned the platform. It was corporate M&A theater.

### The fire sale proved demand at the right price

At $99, TouchPads flew off shelves. The problem wasn't the product — it was the price and HP's lack of commitment.
