# Google Health

Google's personal health record service that aimed to centralize users' medical data. Shut down after four years due to lack of adoption, privacy concerns, and Google's inability to build trust in healthcare.

## The Rise

**2008-05** — Announced at Google I/O by Marissa Mayer

**2008-05** — Partnerships announced with CVS, Walgreens, and Quest Diagnostics

## The Fall

**2010-01** — Usage remains far below projections; health data import is cumbersome

**2011-06** — Google announces Health will shut down in 2012

**2012-01** — Officially shut down; user data available for download until 2013

## Key Lessons

### Healthcare requires trust Google didn't have

Users were unwilling to give their most sensitive data to an advertising company. Privacy concerns were insurmountable.

### Data portability is a nightmare in healthcare

Medical records were scattered across incompatible systems. Importing them into Google Health was manual and frustrating.

### Google tries healthcare every few years and fails

Google Health (2008), Google Fit (2014), Verily (2015), Google Health again (2021). The pattern suggests Google doesn't understand healthcare.
