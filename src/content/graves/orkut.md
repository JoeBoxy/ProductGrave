# Orkut

Google's first foray into social networking, created by a Google engineer as a 20% project. Dominated in Brazil and India but failed to gain global traction against Facebook.

## The Rise

**2004-01** — Created by Google engineer Orkut Büyükkökten as a 20% project

**2004-08** — Opens to public; invite-only system creates early exclusivity

**2007-01** — Brazil becomes largest market with over 60% of users

## The Fall

**2008-01** — India overtakes Brazil as largest user base

**2012-06** — Google+ launches; Orkut starved of resources

**2014-06** — Google announces Orkut will shut down

**2014-09** — Officially shuts down; data archive provided to users

## Key Lessons

### Regional dominance is not global dominance

Orkut owned Brazil and India but was irrelevant in the US and Europe. When Facebook expanded globally, Orkut had no defense.

### 20% projects rarely become core products

Orkut was a side project that Google never seriously invested in. Without executive sponsorship, it was always going to wither.

### Social networks need network effects everywhere

Brazilian users couldn't meaningfully connect with Indian users. Orkut's communities were siloed by geography and language.
