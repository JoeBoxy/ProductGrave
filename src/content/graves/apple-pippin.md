# Apple Pippin

Apple's attempt to enter the gaming console market, a multimedia player based on Mac OS technology. Manufactured by Bandai for the Japanese market. Failed spectacularly against Sony PlayStation and Nintendo 64, selling only 42,000 units.

## The Rise

**1994-01** — Apple begins Pippin project as a low-cost Mac for the living room

**1996-03** — Pippin Atmark launches in Japan (Bandai) for $600

**1996-09** — Pippin @WORLD launches in US for $599

## The Fall

**1996-12** — Only 42,000 units sold in Japan; 2,000 in US

**1997-01** — Bandai stops production; developers abandon platform

**1997-12** — Apple kills Pippin program entirely

**1999-01** — Remaining inventory liquidated at $100 or less

## Key Lessons

### $600 was insane for a 1996 console

PlayStation launched at $299 and Nintendo 64 at $199. Pippin cost $600 and was less powerful. Price killed it before anyone considered the software library.

### A game console lives or dies by its games

Pippin had almost no exclusive titles. Why buy a Pippin when PlayStation had Final Fantasy, Metal Gear Solid, and Tomb Raider?

### Apple + gaming didn't work until the iPhone

Apple tried gaming with Pippin (1996) and failed. It wasn't until the App Store (2008) that Apple accidentally conquered gaming. Different product, different era.
