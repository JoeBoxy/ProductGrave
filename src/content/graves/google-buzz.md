# Google Buzz

Google's first attempt at building a social network inside Gmail. Launched with a massive privacy scandal by making users' email contacts public by default, and was killed after settling an FTC complaint.

## The Rise

**2010-02** — Launched inside Gmail to 176 million users automatically

**2010-02** — Privacy scandal erupts; users' frequent contacts made public without consent

**2010-02** — Class-action lawsuit filed; Google faces global backlash

## The Fall

**2010-09** — FTC investigates; Google settles for privacy policy changes

**2011-06** — Google+ launches; Buzz immediately marked for death

**2011-12** — Officially shut down; all data migrated to Google Drive

## Key Lessons

### Opt-out is not consent

Forcing Buzz on every Gmail user and making contacts public by default was a catastrophic privacy violation that destroyed trust.

### Don't launch social features without understanding social dynamics

Google treated Buzz as a technical feature rollout. It didn't understand that exposing personal relationships requires careful consent.

### Failed experiments poison the well for future products

Buzz's privacy disaster made users deeply skeptical of Google+. The stain on Google's social credibility lasted for years.
