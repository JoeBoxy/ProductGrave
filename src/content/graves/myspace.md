# MySpace

Once the most visited website in the US, MySpace defined a generation of social networking with customizable profiles and music discovery — then was crushed by Facebook's cleaner UX and News Feed.

## The Rise

**2003-08** — Launched by Tom Anderson and Chris DeWolfe

**2005-07** — Acquired by News Corporation for $580 million

**2006-06** — Surpasses Google as most visited website in the US

## The Fall

**2008-04** — Facebook surpasses MySpace in worldwide unique visitors

**2010-10** — Major redesign attempt fails to stop user exodus

**2011-06** — Sold to Specific Media for $35 million; Justin Timberlake takes stake

## Key Lessons

### Clean UX beats customization

MySpace let users destroy their profiles with HTML and auto-playing music. Facebook's uniform, clean design won.

### Real identity matters

MySpace was pseudonymous and band-centric. Facebook's real-name policy built trust and meaningful connections.

### Corporate ownership can kill culture

News Corp prioritized ad revenue over product innovation. The platform became bloated and directionless.
