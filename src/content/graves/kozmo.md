# Kozmo.com

An urban delivery service that promised free one-hour delivery of movies, snacks, and convenience items with no minimum order. Raised $250M and expanded to 10 cities before the dot-com crash killed it.

## The Rise

**1998-01** — Founded by Joseph Park and Yong Kang in New York City

**1999-01** — Expands to Boston, San Francisco, Seattle, and Washington DC

**1999-06** — Announces 10-city expansion plan; hires hundreds of couriers

## The Fall

**2000-01** — Partners with Starbucks for in-store delivery pickup points

**2000-03** — Dot-com crash begins; funding dries up

**2001-04** — Shuts down operations; lays off all 1,100 employees

## Key Lessons

### Free delivery with no minimum is a money pit

Kozmo lost money on every single delivery. The average order was $8 with a delivery cost of $10+. Growth meant bigger losses.

### Dot-com timing was everything

Kozmo might have survived with better unit economics, but the 2000 market crash cut off all funding before it could find a path to profitability.

### Urban density is required for delivery

Kozmo worked in Manhattan but struggled in sprawled cities. The model required extreme population density to minimize delivery costs.
