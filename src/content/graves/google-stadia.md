# Google Stadia

Google's cloud gaming service that let users stream AAA games to any screen without a console. Despite impressive technology, it failed because gamers preferred owning games on Steam/PlayStation, and Google's commitment wavered.

## The Rise

**2019-03** — Announced at GDC with Phil Harrison leading the charge

**2019-11** — Launches with 22 games; requires full game purchases on top of subscription

**2020-01** — First-party game studios announced to develop Stadia exclusives

## The Fall

**2021-02** — Google shuts down its internal game development studios before releasing any games

**2022-01** — Reports emerge that Google is winding down Stadia investment

**2022-09** — Google announces Stadia will shut down in January 2023; offers full refunds

**2023-01** — Stadia servers go dark; technology repurposed for YouTube and Google Cloud

## Key Lessons

### Gamers care about ownership, not just access

Stadia charged full price for games you didn't own. When the service died, users lost everything. Steam and consoles offered permanence.

### Don't enter a market half-heartedly

Google shut down its game studios before they shipped a single title. The message to developers and gamers was clear: we don't believe in this.

### Cloud latency matters for competitive gaming

For casual games, streaming works. For competitive multiplayer, even 50ms of latency is unacceptable. Stadia couldn't solve physics.
