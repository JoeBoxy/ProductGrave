# Better Place

An ambitious electric vehicle infrastructure company that promised to solve range anxiety through automated battery swap stations. Raised $850M but failed because automakers refused to adopt its proprietary standard.

## The Rise

**2007-10** — Founded by Shai Agassi after leaving SAP board

**2008-01** — Announces vision: Israel and Denmark as first markets

**2009-01** — Raises $200M Series B; partners with Renault for Fluence ZE sedan

## The Fall

**2011-01** — First battery swap station opens in Israel

**2012-01** — Renault sells only 1,000 Fluence ZE cars vs. 100,000 projected

**2012-10** — Shai Agassi ousted as CEO by board

**2013-05** — Files for liquidation in Israel; assets sold for pennies on the dollar

## Key Lessons

### Infrastructure without adoption is worthless

Better Place built swap stations before there were cars to use them. It was a Field of Dreams strategy that didn't work.

### Don't bet against the charging standard

Battery swapping required automakers to adopt a proprietary battery design. No major manufacturer was willing to cede control.

### Hero founders can become liabilities

Shai Agassi was a visionary speaker but a poor operator. His refusal to pivot from the swap model led the board to fire him.
