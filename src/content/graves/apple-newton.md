# Apple Newton

Apple's pioneering personal digital assistant (PDA), one of the first handheld devices with handwriting recognition. Launched under John Sculley but suffered from poor handwriting recognition, high price, and bulkiness. Killed by Steve Jobs upon his return to Apple.

## The Rise

**1987-01** — Concept originated by John Sculley; 'Knowledge Navigator' vision

**1993-08** — Newton MessagePad 100 launches at $699

**1993-08** — Doonesbury comic strip mocks Newton's handwriting recognition

**1994-03** — MessagePad 110 improves form factor but still expensive

## The Fall

**1996-01** — eMate 300 targets education market; limited success

**1997-07** — Steve Jobs returns to Apple; Newton immediately marked for death

**1998-02** — Newton officially discontinued

**2007-01** — iPhone launches; many Newton concepts finally realized correctly

## Key Lessons

### Being too early is the same as being wrong

Newton had the right vision — a handheld computer with natural input. But 1993 technology couldn't deliver on the promise. The iPhone, 14 years later, proved the concept.

### Handwriting recognition must work flawlessly or not at all

Newton's 'Calligrapher' recognized handwriting poorly. The Doonesbury strip became iconic. One bad feature can destroy a product's reputation.

### Steve Jobs kills products without sentiment

Jobs returned and immediately killed Newton despite a loyal user base. He believed in focus and Newton distracted from Apple's core mission.
