# Grooveshark

A music streaming service that operated in a legal gray area for nearly a decade by allowing users to upload and share music. Eventually crushed by lawsuits from all major record labels.

## The Rise

**2006-03** — Founded by University of Florida students

**2009-01** — Gains significant traction as free alternative to iTunes

**2010-01** — Universal Music Group files first major lawsuit

## The Fall

**2011-11** — EMI signs licensing deal but later sues for breach

**2013-01** — Google and Apple remove Grooveshark apps from stores due to label pressure

**2014-09** — UMG wins summary judgment; damages could reach $736M

**2015-04** — Shuts down immediately; assets transferred to RIAA as part of settlement

## Key Lessons

### Gray-area business models eventually turn black

Grooveshark operated for nine years without proper licenses. The longer it survived, the bigger the eventual judgment.

### Don't fight an entire industry

When all four major labels plus the RIAA unite against you, no amount of user love or legal maneuvering can save you.

### Cofounder tragedy underscores the human cost

Josh Greenberg died at 28, just months before the shutdown. The stress of years of litigation took a severe toll on the team.
