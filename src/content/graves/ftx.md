## The Autopsy

FTX went from a $32 billion valuation to bankruptcy in roughly 72 hours. It wasn't a slow decline. It was a controlled demolition triggered by a single tweet.

### What Killed It

**1. The FTT Alchemy**

Alameda Research, SBF's trading firm, held billions in FTT — a cryptocurrency created and controlled by FTX itself. FTT had no intrinsic value beyond FTX's promise to buy it back. This meant FTX's "sister company" was collateralized by an asset that would become worthless if FTX failed. It was a circular dependency designed to explode.

When CoinDesk published Alameda's balance sheet on November 2, 2022, the market saw the con. FTT's price began falling. When CZ of Binance publicly announced he would dump his FTT holdings, the bank run began.

**2. The Missing Billions**

As FTX unraveled, it became clear that customer deposits had been used to fund Alameda's risky trades, political donations, and luxury real estate. The exchange didn't have the assets it claimed. Billions in customer funds remain missing. This wasn't a liquidity crisis. It was theft.

**3. The Due Diligence Theater**

Sequoia Capital, SoftBank, Tiger Global, and dozens of other elite investors put over $1.8 billion into FTX. Many later admitted they had never seen a complete balance sheet. SBF's reputation as a genius trader and effective altruist was enough. In a bull market, greed replaces governance.

**4. No Adults in the Room**

FTX had no board of directors in any traditional sense. SBF, Gary Wang, and Nishad Singh made all decisions. There was no CFO with real authority, no risk committee, and no separation between FTX (the exchange) and Alameda (the trading firm). The organizational chart was a straight line to SBF.

### The Afterlife

SBF was convicted on seven counts of fraud in November 2023 and sentenced to 25 years in prison. FTX's bankruptcy proceedings continue, with customers hoping to recover cents on the dollar. The collapse accelerated crypto regulation worldwide and destroyed the credibility of "effective altruism" as a marketing tool. In death, FTX became the Lehman Brothers of crypto — the moment the industry could no longer pretend it was just tech.
