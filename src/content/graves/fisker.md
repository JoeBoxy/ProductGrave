# Fisker Automotive

A luxury electric vehicle startup that produced the stunning Fisker Karma sedan. Destroyed by battery supplier failures, Hurricane Sandy flooding its inventory, and the US government's withdrawal of loan guarantees.

## The Rise

**2007-09** — Founded by legendary car designer Henrik Fisker

**2009-09** — Secures $529M US government loan guarantee

**2011-10** — Karma production begins; priced at $103,000

**2012-03** — Consumer Reports test car breaks down during evaluation

## The Fall

**2012-10** — Hurricane Sandy floods port; destroys 300 Karmas worth $32M

**2012-12** — Battery supplier A123 Systems goes bankrupt

**2013-04** — US Department of Energy freezes remaining loan funds

**2013-11** — Files for bankruptcy; assets sold to Chinese auto parts company

## Key Lessons

### Single-source suppliers are single points of failure

Fisker relied entirely on A123 Systems for batteries. When A123 went bankrupt, Fisker had no alternative supplier.

### Government loans come with strings and political risk

The DOE loan guarantee helped Fisker start but became a liability when political winds shifted. The freeze killed the company.

### Beauty is not enough for cars

The Karma was gorgeous but unreliable and expensive. In the luxury market, Tesla proved that technology and performance beat design alone.
