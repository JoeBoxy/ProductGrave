# AltaVista

The dominant search engine of the 1990s that indexed the entire web when competitors could only handle subsets. Bought by Yahoo in 2003 and gradually dismantled as Yahoo prioritized its own search technology.

## The Rise

**1995-12** — Launched by Digital Equipment Corporation researchers

**1996-01** — First search engine to index 20+ million pages

**1998-01** — Peak popularity; one of the most visited sites on the web

## The Fall

**1998-09** — Google launches; begins eating AltaVista's market share

**2003-04** — Acquired by Overture Services; then by Yahoo

**2010-01** — Yahoo begins redirecting AltaVista queries to Yahoo Search

**2013-07** — Officially shut down by Yahoo

## Key Lessons

### Search is winner-take-all

AltaVista had the technology and the brand. But Google's PageRank algorithm and clean interface were simply better. In search, #2 is worthless.

### Corporate parents kill innovation

Owned first by DEC, then Compaq, then Overture, then Yahoo — each owner had less interest in search. AltaVista was a pawn in corporate chess.

### Don't diversify away from your core

AltaVista tried to become a portal (email, shopping, news) instead of focusing on search. The distraction allowed Google to overtake it.
