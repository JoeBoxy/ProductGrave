# Nokia N-Gage

Nokia's attempt to combine a mobile phone and handheld gaming console. Widely mocked for its awkward 'taco' shape, expensive games, and poor game library. Sold only 3 million units versus Nintendo DS's 150 million.

## The Rise

**2003-02** — Announced at E3 gaming expo

**2003-10** — Launches at $299; requires games on MMC cards

**2003-11** — Mocked globally for requiring users to hold it sideways against their head to make calls

## The Fall

**2004-01** — Price dropped to $199 after poor holiday sales

**2004-05** — Nokia redesigns to N-Gage QD with speaker relocation

**2005-01** — Only 2 million units sold in first 18 months

**2006-11** — N-Gage gaming platform discontinued as standalone hardware

## Key Lessons

### Form factor matters more than features

The N-Gage was technically capable but ergonomically disastrous. Holding a phone sideways to your face is a design crime.

### Games sell consoles, not hardware specs

N-Gage launched with few compelling titles. Nintendo DS had Mario, Pokemon, and Zelda. Nokia had Snake and Tomb Raider ports.

### Don't enter a market dominated by an entrenched player

Nintendo owned handheld gaming. Nokia thought its phone expertise would translate. It didn't — gaming and phones require different DNA.
