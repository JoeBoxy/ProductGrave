# Ouya

A Kickstarter darling that promised to disrupt the gaming console industry with an affordable Android-based box. Raised $8.5 million from crowdfunding but delivered underwhelming hardware and a sparse game library.

## The Rise

**2012-07** — Kickstarter campaign launches; raises $8.5M from 63,000 backers

**2013-03** — Consoles begin shipping to backers; controller receives terrible reviews

**2013-06** — Retail launch at Best Buy, Target, and GameStop

## The Fall

**2013-12** — Reports indicate sales are well below projections

**2014-01** — Ouya attempts 'Free the Games' fund to attract developers; accused of fraud

**2015-06** — Razer acquires Ouya software and staff; hardware discontinued

## Key Lessons

### Hardware is hard

The Ouya controller was universally panned for poor ergonomics and lag. In gaming, the controller IS the product.

### Kickstarter hype ≠ product success

$8.5M in crowdfunding created massive expectations. When the product underdelivered, the backlash was brutal.

### Content is king

A console without great exclusive games is just a box. Ouya had no must-play titles to justify its existence.
