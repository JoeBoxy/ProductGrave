# Hello Sense

A sleep tracking system consisting of a beautiful bedside orb and a clip-on 'Sleep Pill.' Raised $2.4M on Kickstarter and $40M total but failed to compete with Apple Watch, Fitbit, and smartphone sleep apps.

## The Rise

**2012-08** — Founded by Thiel Fellow James Proud

**2014-08** — Kickstarter raises $2.4M from 19,000 backers

**2015-02** — Sense ships to backers; praised for design but criticized for accuracy

## The Fall

**2015-11** — Raises $30M Series B led by Temasek

**2016-06** — Launches Sense with Voice (2nd generation) with Amazon Alexa integration

**2017-02** — Rumors of financial trouble emerge

**2017-06** — Hello Inc. shuts down; assets sold; many backers never receive products

## Key Lessons

### Single-purpose hardware loses to general-purpose devices

Sense did one thing: track sleep. But Apple Watch, Fitbit, and even phones did sleep tracking plus 100 other things. Users didn't want a dedicated sleep orb.

### Beautiful design doesn't justify dedicated hardware

Sense was gorgeous. But a $150 orb on your nightstand is hard to justify when a $10 phone app does 80% of the same job.

### Thiel Fellowship doesn't guarantee success

James Proud was a Thiel Fellow who dropped out of college. The credential attracted investors but didn't translate to business execution.
