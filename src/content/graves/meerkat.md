# Meerkat

The first viral live-streaming app that let users broadcast directly to Twitter. Dominated SXSW 2015 before Twitter acquired Periscope and shut down Meerkat's access to its social graph.

## The Rise

**2015-02** — Launched at SXSW; becomes breakout hit of the festival

**2015-03** — Twitter announces acquisition of Periscope — a direct competitor

**2015-03** — Twitter cuts off Meerkat's access to the social graph API

## The Fall

**2015-03** — Raises $14M from Greylock and others despite Twitter hostility

**2016-03** — Pivots to 'Houseparty' group video chat app

**2016-10** — Meerkat app officially shut down

## Key Lessons

### Building on another platform is renting, not owning

Meerkat's entire viral loop depended on Twitter. When Twitter pulled API access, the product lost its distribution overnight.

### First mover advantage is overstated

Meerkat was first to viral mobile live streaming. Periscope was second but had Twitter's distribution. Being first didn't matter.

### Know when to pivot

The team saw the writing on the wall and pivoted to Houseparty, which was eventually acquired by Epic Games for a reported $35M.
