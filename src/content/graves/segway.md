# Segway

The self-balancing personal transporter that was hyped as revolutionary urban transportation. Despite $200M in funding and Steve Jobs-level predictions, it never found mass adoption outside niche markets.

## The Rise

**2001-12** — Launched with massive hype; called 'as important as the PC'

**2002-01** — Amazon founder Jeff Bezos and Apple CEO Steve Jobs reportedly shown prototypes pre-launch

**2003-06** — Company sold to a group of investors for undisclosed sum

## The Fall

**2009-12** — British owner Jimi Heselden dies after driving Segway off cliff

**2013-01** — Sold to Summit Strategic Investments after years of declining sales

**2015-04** — Chinese company Ninebot (backed by Xiaomi and Sequoia) acquires Segway

**2020-06** — Segway PT production discontinued entirely; brand continues under Ninebot

## Key Lessons

### Revolutionary tech needs a real problem

Segway solved 'walking is hard' — a problem that didn't exist for most people. Without a compelling use case, it was an expensive toy.

### Regulation kills personal mobility

Segways were banned on sidewalks in many cities and not street-legal. No infrastructure meant nowhere to ride.

### Price matters

At $5,000+ initially, Segway was too expensive for consumers. By the time price dropped, e-scooters and e-bikes had taken the market.
