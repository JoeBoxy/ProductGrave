# Google Reader

The beloved RSS feed reader that helped a generation curate the web. Killed by Google in 2013 to redirect resources toward the failed Google+ social network, sparking a decade of internet resentment.

## The Rise

**2005-10** — Launched by Chris Wetherell and Jason Shellen as a 20% project

**2006-01** — Crashes on launch day due to overwhelming traffic

**2007-10** — Becomes one of Google's most beloved products despite small team

## The Fall

**2011-01** — Put into 'maintenance mode' as team members pulled to Google+ project

**2013-03** — Google announces shutdown, buried on the same day Pope Francis was elected

**2013-07** — Officially goes offline; millions of users scramble to find alternatives

## Key Lessons

### Internal politics kill good products

Reader was killed not because it failed, but because Google needed engineers for Google+. A small, beloved product lost to a mega-project.

### Don't confuse features with products

Google executives always saw Reader as 'a feature, not a product.' But to users, it was the center of their information diet.

### Loyalty doesn't protect you from corporate strategy

Reader had millions of devoted users. Google's 'declining use' excuse ignored how deeply embedded it was in power users' workflows.
