# Diaspora

A privacy-focused, decentralized social network launched as a Kickstarter project in response to Facebook's growing power. Raised $200K but struggled with technical complexity and the tragic death of co-founder Ilya Zhitomirskiy.

## The Rise

**2010-04** — Concept born in response to a NYU computer science class privacy lecture

**2010-09** — Kickstarter raises $200,642 from 6,479 backers

**2010-11** — Alpha code released; developers can run their own 'pods'

## The Fall

**2011-11** — Co-founder Ilya Zhitomirskiy dies by suicide at age 22

**2012-08** — Core team moves on to other projects; Diaspora becomes community-run

**2014-01** — Diaspora Foundation takes over governance

## Key Lessons

### Decentralization is technically and socially hard

Running your own 'pod' required technical skill most users lacked. The promise of decentralization became a barrier to adoption.

### Privacy alone doesn't drive adoption

Diaspora's privacy features were compelling to activists but irrelevant to mainstream users who valued convenience over control.

### Open source social networks face the empty room problem

Your friends needed to join Diaspora for it to be useful. But they were all on Facebook. Network effects are nearly impossible to overcome.
