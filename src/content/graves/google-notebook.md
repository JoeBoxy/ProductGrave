# Google Notebook

Google's web-based note-taking and research tool that let users clip content from web pages into organized notebooks. Launched as a Labs project but shut down in 2011, with data exported to Google Docs. The concept later resurfaced as Google Keep.

## The Rise

**2006-05** — Launched as Google Labs experiment

**2007-01** — Gains loyal user base among researchers and students

**2008-01** — Competition from Evernote and Microsoft OneNote intensifies

## The Fall

**2009-01** — Google stops active development; no new features

**2011-01** — Google announces Notebook will shut down

**2011-07** — Officially shut down; user data exported to Google Docs

**2013-03** — Google Keep launches — a simpler, mobile-first replacement

## Key Lessons

### Google's product strategy is replace, not iterate

Instead of improving Notebook, Google killed it and built Keep from scratch. Users lost years of organized research in the transition.

### Browser extensions are hard to monetize

Notebook was a free browser tool with no ads. Google couldn't justify the engineering cost against near-zero revenue.

### Evernote won by being cross-platform and committed

While Google wavered, Evernote built a robust, cross-platform note-taking ecosystem. Commitment beats a better-funded but distracted competitor.
