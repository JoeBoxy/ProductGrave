# Microsoft Kin

A social-media-focused phone aimed at teenagers, created by the team Microsoft acquired from Danger (makers of the Sidekick). Lacked an app store, games, and instant messaging. Discontinued after just six weeks.

## The Rise

**2008-02** — Microsoft acquires Danger Inc. for $500M

**2009-01** — Danger team begins Project 'Pink' — a social phone for teens

**2010-04** — Kin ONE and Kin TWO launch on Verizon

## The Fall

**2010-04** — Reviews pan the lack of apps, games, Twitter client, and instant messaging

**2010-04** — Requires expensive $30/month data plan despite limited functionality

**2010-06** — Microsoft officially discontinues Kin; remaining stock sent to Verizon stores

## Key Lessons

### Know your audience

Kin was marketed to teens but lacked the features teens actually wanted: apps, games, instant messaging, and a reasonable data plan.

### Don't overpay for teams then ignore their expertise

Microsoft paid $500M for Danger and its talented team. Then internal politics with Windows Phone team led to Kin being built on Windows CE instead of Danger's proven OS.

### 48 days is not a product lifecycle

Kin holds the record for one of the shortest-lived consumer electronics products in history. It was dead before most people knew it existed.
