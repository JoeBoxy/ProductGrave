# Saygus

A Utah-based smartphone company that promised impossibly advanced specs — including 320GB storage and a built-in projector. Took millions in pre-orders over eight years but never delivered a product. Founder later indicted for fraud.

## The Rise

**2009-01** — Founded by Chad Sayers

**2015-01** — Announces V² smartphone with absurd specs at CES; gains massive media attention

**2015-03** — Opens pre-orders; thousands of customers pay $600+ deposits

**2015-06** — First shipping delays announced

## The Fall

**2016-01** — Sayers claims phones are 'in production' but provides no evidence

**2016-12** — FCC documents reveal Saygus never submitted phones for certification

**2017-01** — Company effectively dead; customers report no refunds

**2020-01** — Chad Sayers indicted by DOJ for wire fraud related to investor deception

## Key Lessons

### If specs sound too good to be true, they are

320GB in a smartphone in 2015 was physically impossible. Saygus used fantasy specs to lure pre-orders from tech enthusiasts.

### Never pre-order from unknown hardware companies

Saygus took millions in deposits and delivered nothing. Without escrow or consumer protections, crowdfunding pre-orders are high-risk.

### CES demos can be pure theater

Saygus won 'Best of CES' awards with non-functional prototypes. Trade show accolades mean nothing if the product doesn't ship.
