# Nokia Phones

The once-dominant mobile phone manufacturer that failed to adapt to the smartphone revolution. From 40% global market share to selling its phone business to Microsoft for a fraction of its peak value.

## The Rise

**1992-01** — Nokia focuses on mobile phones after divesting other businesses

**1998-01** — Becomes world's largest mobile phone manufacturer

**2007-01** — iPhone launches; Nokia dismisses touchscreen smartphones

## The Fall

**2008-01** — Symbian OS loses ground to iOS and Android

**2011-02** — Partnership with Microsoft to use Windows Phone OS announced

**2013-09** — Microsoft acquires Nokia phone business for $7.2 billion

**2014-04** — Nokia phone division formally absorbed into Microsoft Mobile

## Key Lessons

### Disruption comes from outside your industry

Nokia saw itself competing with Motorola and Sony Ericsson. The real threat was Apple, a computer company.

### Software eats hardware

Nokia made great phones but terrible software. When the value shifted to apps and ecosystems, hardware excellence wasn't enough.

### Sunk cost fallacy kills giants

Nokia doubled down on Symbian and then Windows Phone because of prior investment, instead of adopting Android early.
