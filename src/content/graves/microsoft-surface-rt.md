# Microsoft Surface RT

Microsoft's first tablet running Windows RT, an ARM-based OS that couldn't run traditional Windows applications. Confused consumers, sold poorly, and resulted in a $900 million write-down.

## The Rise

**2012-06** — Announced by Steve Ballmer at mysterious Los Angeles press event

**2012-10** — Launches alongside Windows 8; priced at $499 (same as iPad)

**2012-11** — Reviews criticize lack of apps and confusing Windows RT vs Windows 8 distinction

## The Fall

**2013-01** — Microsoft cuts Surface RT orders by half due to poor sales

**2013-07** — Microsoft takes $900M write-down on unsold Surface RT inventory

**2013-09** — Price dropped to $349; Surface 2 announced but Surface RT abandoned

## Key Lessons

### Incompatible branding kills products

Consumers bought Surface RT thinking it ran Windows apps. It didn't. The 'Windows' brand was a liability when the product couldn't deliver Windows compatibility.

### App ecosystems are everything in mobile

Windows RT launched with a barren app store. No Instagram, no Snapchat, no proper Office. An iPad competitor without apps is just a expensive web browser.

### $900M teaches humility

The write-down was one of Microsoft's biggest hardware failures. It forced the company to rethink its entire device strategy and eventually led to Ballmer's departure.
