# Coolest Cooler

A Kickstarter phenomenon that raised $13.3 million — the most-funded campaign at the time. A cooler with built-in blender, Bluetooth speaker, and USB charger that became a case study in crowdfunding over-promise.

## The Rise

**2013-11** — First Kickstarter campaign fails; raises only $125,000 of $125,000 goal

**2014-07** — Relaunches on Kickstarter; raises $13.3M from 62,000 backers

**2015-01** — Manufacturing delays begin; costs far exceed projections

## The Fall

**2015-07** — Backers still waiting; company begins selling coolers on Amazon before fulfilling Kickstarter orders

**2016-01** — Admits it needs additional $15M to fulfill all backer orders

**2017-01** — Thousands of backers still without coolers two years after campaign

**2019-12** — Company goes bankrupt; many backers never receive products or refunds

## Key Lessons

### Kickstarter is not a store

Backers treated it like a pre-order platform. But crowdfunding projects face real manufacturing risks that retail doesn't.

### Hardware margins are brutal

The Coolest Cooler was underpriced at $185. By the time manufacturing scaled, each unit cost more to make than the selling price.

### Feature creep kills products

Blender + speaker + charger + LED light + bottle opener. Each feature added cost, complexity, and failure points. A cooler should keep things cold.
