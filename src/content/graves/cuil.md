# Cuil

A search engine founded by ex-Google engineers that claimed to index more pages than Google. Launched with massive hype but delivered poor results, a confusing interface, and a bizarre name pronunciation ('cool').

## The Rise

**2006-01** — Founded by Anna Patterson and Tom Costello, both ex-Google search engineers

**2007-01** — Raises $33M from Greylock Partners and others

**2008-07** — Launches claiming to index 120 billion pages — 3x Google at the time

## The Fall

**2008-07** — First searches return irrelevant results; pornographic images appear in innocuous queries

**2008-08** — Traffic plummets as users return to Google

**2009-01** — Lays off staff; pivots to less ambitious search features

**2010-09** — Shuts down; assets acquired by Google (irony noted)

## Key Lessons

### Indexing more pages doesn't mean better search

Cuil's core claim was indexing 120B pages. But search quality depends on ranking relevance, not index size. Google won on quality, not quantity.

### Ex-Google engineers aren't guaranteed to beat Google

Cuil's founders were brilliant Google engineers. But they lacked Google's data, infrastructure, and distribution. Talent alone isn't enough.

### A confusing name kills word-of-mouth

Nobody knew how to pronounce 'Cuil' ('cool'? 'quill'?). The bizarre name and spider-themed imagery made the product feel alien rather than approachable.
