# Zune

Microsoft's portable media player and online music service, launched as a direct competitor to Apple's iPod and iTunes. Despite superior hardware in some generations, it never gained meaningful market share and was discontinued after five years.

## The Rise

**2006-09** — Zune 30GB launched in US; brown color mocked relentlessly

**2007-11** — Zune 2.0 launched with flash models and improved software

**2008-09** — Zune Pass subscription service offers unlimited downloads for $14.99/month

## The Fall

**2009-09** — Zune HD launches with OLED screen; praised by critics but ignored by consumers

**2010-10** — Microsoft shifts Zune team to Windows Phone; no new hardware planned

**2011-10** — Zune hardware discontinued; Zune Pass service killed in 2012

**2012-06** — Zune brand fully retired; services absorbed into Xbox Music

## Key Lessons

### You can't beat Apple at its own game with a follower product

By 2006, iPod had 75% market share and iTunes was the default music store. Zune was better hardware in some ways but offered no compelling reason to switch ecosystems.

### The Zune Pass was actually innovative but too late

All-you-can-eat music subscriptions at $15/month was ahead of Spotify's US launch. But Zune hardware sales were too small to make the service viable.

### Microsoft's consumer brand was toxic in the 2000s

The brown Zune became a cultural punchline. Microsoft was seen as the boring corporate choice versus Apple's cool factor. Brand matters in consumer hardware.
